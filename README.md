Baby Wall Protector

A safety-based design thinking project developed to reduce baby injuries caused by accidental collisions with sharp wall edges and hard surfaces inside homes.

The Baby Wall Protector is designed using soft protective materials that can be attached to walls, corners, and furniture edges to provide a safer environment for infants and toddlers.

---

Project Overview

Babies and toddlers are naturally active and curious while learning to crawl, walk, and play. During this stage, they are more likely to hit walls, sharp corners, or hard furniture edges, which may lead to injuries.

The Baby Wall Protector is a simple and effective safety product designed to minimize such accidents by providing soft cushioning protection.

This project focuses on:

- Child safety
- Injury prevention
- Easy installation
- Cost-effective protection
- User-friendly design

---

Problem Statement

Many babies get injured at home due to:

- Sharp wall corners
- Hard furniture edges
- Slippery movement near walls
- Lack of protective cushioning

Parents often worry about the safety of babies during play and movement inside the house.

---

Proposed Solution

The Baby Wall Protector provides soft protective padding that can be attached to:

- Wall corners
- Table edges
- Bed corners
- Furniture sides

The product absorbs impact and reduces injury risk when babies accidentally collide with hard surfaces.

Working Process

1. Identify unsafe wall corners and edges
2. Measure required protection area
3. Cut protective foam material
4. Attach cushioning using adhesive
5. Ensure proper fitting and safety
6. Test impact protection

---

Design Thinking Process

1. Empathize

Understanding the safety concerns of parents regarding baby injuries at home.

2. Define

Identifying dangerous wall corners and furniture edges as the main problem.

3. Ideate

Generating ideas for soft protective coverings to reduce injuries.

4. Prototype

Creating a simple protective model using foam and cushioning materials.

5. Test

Testing the protector for comfort, flexibility, and impact reduction.
